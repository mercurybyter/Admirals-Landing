The Town of Farragut

The Founder of Farragut

David Glasgow Farragut was a U.S. admiral born on July 5th, 1801. He is known for his many Union naval victories during the American Civil War, from 1861-1865. As a youth, Farragut was befriended and adopted by Captain David Porter, whom he served under aboard the frigate Essex during the War of 1812. Amongst his victories, Farragut had destroyed much of the Confederate river squadron and caused the surrender of many forts, including Fort Morgan. Farragut was especially well known for his line "Damn the torpedoes, full speed ahead!". He was made a full admiral in 1866, four years before his death in 1870.

The Beginning

Farragut was created as a town on January 16th, 1980. The first Board of Mayor and Aldermen were elected on April 1st of that same year.

Historical Architecture

The Campbell Station Inn, created in the early 1800s, served as a resting place for many, from families and hunter to even President Andrew Jackson and Tennessee Governor John Sevier!

Civil War History

The Battle of Campbell Station took place on November 16th, 1863. This battle was fought by Burnside's Union forces, who defeated Longstreet's Confederate forces. This important fight occurred on the land that currently surrounds Farragut's Town Hall. A Civil War Trails Historical Marker was dedicated in 2010 to these grounds to commemorate this event.
